We were fine, until 'Goodbye' became the line

We were something in between,

More than friends, but less than what love means.

There was a spark we never named,

We are a story that stayed unnamed.


You laughed with me like I was home,

You made me believe I’d never be alone.

It felt like love, it felt so right,

Yet it faded away on a random Thursday night.


I thought I knew you, every part of you,

But now I’m left asking, “Who are you?”

You were so afraid that I might leave,

But in the end, it was you who chose to leave.


You were never mine, yet I grieve,

For a love I held but can't receive.

How do I unlove what I can’t define,

When all I wanted was to call you mine?


I drown in moments we never owned,

In you, where quietly my heart had grown.

You were a chapter I couldn’t write,

A stolen dawn that never turned to night.


Now I’m haunted by the nights we shared,

By heavy words that proved you cared.

But if you cared, then tell me why,

You left me broken, you let me cry.


The hardest part I can’t deny,

Is loving someone who was never mine.

I beg my heart to let you go,

But love holds on when the mind says no.


And maybe what hurts is not the end,

But knowing we bent what we couldn’t mend.

Perhaps in another time, another place,

I might have found in you my safe space.
